Rideshare questions

Mileage & taxes for Uber & Lyft drivers.

Can I deduct miles Uber doesn't report?

Generally, all the miles you drive while working as a rideshare contractor — including between trips and while waiting for requests — can be deductible business miles, not just the on-trip miles Uber/Lyft report. You need a log to support them, which Routio keeps automatically. (General information, not tax advice.)

Should I use the standard mileage rate or actual expenses?

Most rideshare drivers use the standard mileage rate (72.5¢/mile for 2026) because it's simpler and often larger. Routio is built around the standard mileage method and gives you the total to claim. A tax professional can confirm what's best for you.

Does it work for both Uber and Lyft (and multi-apping)?

Yes. Routio tracks your driving regardless of which app you're on, so if you run Uber and Lyft together, every working mile is still captured in one place.

Will it drain my battery during long shifts?

Routio uses iOS's efficient motion and location services designed for all-day background use, so it's built to be light on battery across a full shift.
