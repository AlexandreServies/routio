Dasher questions

Mileage & taxes for DoorDash drivers.

Can I deduct miles I drive for DoorDash?

If you're an independent contractor (which Dashers are), the business miles you drive while working are generally deductible at the IRS standard mileage rate — 72.5¢ per mile for 2026. Routio logs them automatically so you have the records to claim them. (This is general information, not tax advice — check with a tax professional.)

Does DoorDash's mileage estimate count?

DoorDash provides a mileage estimate, but it typically only covers active delivery miles and may understate your real total (it often misses the drive to your starting zone and between orders). A dedicated, contemporaneous log like Routio's captures the full picture.

Do I track miles between deliveries and to my zone?

Generally, miles driven while working — including between deliveries and to your starting area — can count as business miles. Routio records the whole shift automatically so nothing slips through.

Do I need to keep a log, or is the deduction enough?

The IRS expects a contemporaneous mileage log to back up the deduction. That's exactly what Routio keeps for you, automatically, with dates, routes, and totals.
